SHARKS

Fun Facts

  1. Diversity:
    • Great White Nomads: Great white sharks are known for their long-distance migrations. They can travel thousands of miles across the ocean, making them true nomads of the sea.

    • Ancient Predators: Sharks have been around for a long time. Some species, like the frilled shark, have remained virtually unchanged for millions of years, showcasing the incredible resilience and adaptability of these creatures.

    • Small but Mighty: The dwarf lanternshark, the world's smallest shark, is about the size of a human hand. Despite its tiny stature, it can emit light to camouflage itself in the deep sea.

  2. Behavior:
    • Social Hammerheads: Hammerhead sharks are known to form schools during the day, displaying a more social behavior than many other shark species.

    • Clever Hunters: Some sharks, like the lemon shark, have been observed using their fins to "herd" schools of fish into tighter groups, making it easier for the shark to catch its prey.

    • Sleep on the Move: Sharks do not sleep in the way humans do. Instead, some species, like the nurse shark, rest by swimming slowly with their eyes partially closed.

  3. Myth-busting:
    • Misunderstood Predators: While sharks are often portrayed as aggressive predators, they are responsible for a small number of attacks on humans compared to other animals like dogs. Most shark species are not interested in humans as prey.

    • No Bones About It: Contrary to popular belief, sharks do not have bones; instead, their skeletons are made of cartilage. This makes them more agile and lighter in the water.

    • Shark Skin Revolution: The skin of sharks has inspired innovations in swimwear design. The tiny, tooth-like structures called denticles on their skin reduce drag, leading to faster and more efficient swimsuit designs for athletes.
